The Philosopher

Plato was an Athenian philosopher during the Classical period in Ancient Greece, founder of the Academy — the first institution of higher learning in the Western world. A student of Socrates and teacher of Aristotle, he laid the foundations of Western philosophy and science.

His works are almost entirely in the form of dialogues, in which Socrates and others discuss ethical, metaphysical, epistemological, and political questions. The theory of Forms, the allegory of the cave, and the concept of Platonic love all originate in his writings, which continue to be studied more than two millennia after his death.
